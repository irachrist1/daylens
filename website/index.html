You're losing hours you can't account for.

Most people have no idea where their day actually went. The apps they think they use and the apps they actually use never match. Manual time-trackers don't get used. Analytics tools that send your data somewhere ask you to trust a company with your entire behavior pattern.

Daylens solves this differently. It runs in the background, watches what you do locally and automatically, and explains it back to you in plain language — powered by AI that's grounded in your real data, not generic productivity advice.

Local-first privacy

No telemetry, no analytics, no cloud sync. All data lives in ~/Library on your Mac.

AI-powered insight

Daily summaries and Q&A grounded in your actual activity data. Works with Claude or OpenAI-compatible APIs.

Multi-browser

Chrome, Arc, Safari, Brave, Edge, and Firefox all feed into the same timeline automatically.

Focus scoring

See whether your day was focused or fragmented, with scores tied to real session data.

Category overrides

Fix miscategorized apps. Your corrections persist and flow through summaries, scores, and AI context.

Full history

Browse any past day, review weekly trends, and export everything to JSON.

How it works

1

Install

Run brew tap irachrist1/daylens && brew install --cask daylens. Or download the DMG from GitHub Releases.

2

Grant permissions

Daylens will ask for Accessibility and Full Disk Access on first launch. Both are required — Accessibility to know which app is active, Full Disk Access to read your browser history databases.

3

Use your Mac normally

Daylens runs quietly in the menu bar. No manual tracking, no timers to start or stop.

4

Open Daylens

See your focus score, today's timeline, top apps, and website activity. Ask the AI anything about your day.
